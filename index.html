The Queen's Bones

A jester with a checkered past, a hero who doesn't believe his own story, a snake that wants to be a dragon, and a queen that won't stay dead. Sword fights, a fire breathing dragon, and a jester will decide what happens after the hero has saved the kingdom.

The Queen's Bones is a fantasy comedy with a dash of horror made for ages 10 and up.

Parents Guide:

Light inappropriate language:

The play contains usage of the word bastard.

Romantic Themes:

There are romantic scenes and light use of euphemism.

Violence:

Light fantasy violence with a sword fight at the end. Some frightening imagery.

Director's Note

I’ve always loved art that can make me laugh and cry. The Queen’s Bones is a special sort of play that has the potential to do both. I knew from early on that striking this balance between sad and funny would be my greatest challenge. It really clicked when I remembered the bed time stories my parents read to me growing up. Some were fun some were sad and others were somewhere in between. It struck me that the most important thing with those stories was the flow of the narrative. So i determined to make the Queen’s Bones feel like a best type of bedtime story that could make you laugh and cry.

- Andrew Elijah Schindler
